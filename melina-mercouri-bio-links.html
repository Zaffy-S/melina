Melina Mercouri

The Last Greek Goddess

Short Bio & Links

Who was Melina?

Melina was born as Maria Amalia Mercouri in Athens on 18 October 1920.
She came from a prominent political family.

She was an actress, a singer, an activist, a politician and one of the Icons in 20th century's Greece, very popular and loved in her country.

As an actress she has nominated and won awards (like Best Actress in Cannes Festival) and had international success in films and theater, with movie hits like “Never on Sunday”, “TopKapi” &  “Illya Darling” in Broadway.

As an singer, among other great tunes, she sang "Ta pedia tou Pirea", a song composed by Michalis Kakogiannis & Manos Hadjidakis, that won the Oscar in 1960 Academy Awards.

As an activist, she started an international campaign against the Greek Junta, while she was exiled from Greece. For her fight against the Military regime, Junta revoked her Greek citizenship. Meline famous reaction was "I was born a Greek and I will die a Greek. They were born fascists and will die fascists".
During her campaign against the Colonels, she was the target of two assassination attempts.

As a politician, she was elected in the Greek Parliament in every election from 1977 to 1993. In 1981, she was the first female Minister of Culture and Sports, a position she would serve (in two terms) for 10 years in total, the longest ever in Greek history. She is considered the best Minister of Culture ever with many achievements and innovations.

She campaigned for the return of the Parthenon Marbles back to Athens. Marbles that were stolen from Acropolis and are now a part of the British Museum collection in London. For this cause, she was the one who started the procedures to build the Acropolis Museum.

The famous American director Jules Dassin, the love of her life, was her second husband. They stayed together for almost 40 years, till her death.

Melina died on 6 March 1994, in New York City, US, from lung cancer. The girl who became famous for "Never on Sunday" died on a Sunday... the irony…
She was the first woman in Greece that got buried with prime minister honors and the people who gathered in her funeral in Athens were estimated to be many hundreds of thousands. 
During the time of her funeral, Broadway theaters had their lights turned off to honor her memory.
